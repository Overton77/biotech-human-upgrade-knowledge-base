# NCT03630419 Mito-Food Plan and Cellular Repair Study

## Study Overview

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Registry | `NCT03630419` |
| Status | `Completed` |
| Phase | `Phase 1 / Phase 2` |
| Enrollment | `5` |
| Conditions | Mild Cognitive Impairment, Alzheimer Disease |
| Sponsor | Perseverance Research Center, LLC |
| Location | Scottsdale, Arizona |
| Timeframe | Start `2018-02-10`, completed `2018-07-28` |

## Intervention Design

- `Mito-Food Plan`: personalized anti-inflammatory diet based on physiological assessment
- `Cellular Repair Therapy`: an adjunctive technology described in the registry as supporting repair of protein structures and maintenance of DNA health
- Single experimental arm: Mito-Food Plan plus cellular repair therapy

## Outcomes

### Primary

- Change in inflammation from baseline to week 12
- Change in `MMSE`
- Change in `MoCA`

### Secondary

- Change in `QOL-AD`

## Publication Trail

- A publication-style article with the matching title appears on `Longdom` and `Bodystat`.
- The article is not indexed in PubMed in this mission.
- The Longdom page contains a mistyped study ID (`NCT0360419`) in the article text, while the actual registry record is `NCT03630419`.

## Key Findings as Reported

- The article says `3` MCI and `2` AD participants were studied.
- It reports improvements in cognitive testing and quality of life along with reduced chronic inflammation.
- Four of the five participants reportedly used adjunctive cellular repair therapy, creating a major attribution problem between diet effects and NanoVi-adjacent therapy effects.

## Strengths

1. The study is real and registered in ClinicalTrials.gov.
2. It targets clinically meaningful outcomes rather than only lab surrogates.
3. The registry/publication pair gives more traceability than a pure marketing summary.

## Limitations

1. Extremely small sample size (`n=5`).
2. Mixed intervention design prevents clean isolation of NanoVi-adjacent effects.
3. The publication channel is weak relative to mainstream indexed literature.
4. No PubMed indexing was found in this mission.

## Evidence Quality Rating

**Level: Preliminary**

Useful as a traceable proof-of-concept, but far too small and confounded to support strong therapeutic claims.
