## Key Observations

- The Eng3 studies page blends multiple evidence classes on one page: peer-reviewed mechanistic work, registry-backed human work, practitioner case material, and company summary statistics.
- The strongest externally verifiable evidence in this mission is the `2022 IJMS` enzyme paper.
- The strongest human-clinical line is the `NCT03630419` MCI/AD study, but it remains small, mixed-intervention, and not resolved to a PubMed-indexed paper.
- The athletic, HRV, oxidative-stress, and immune-response claims may still be real observations, but they remain materially less authoritative until linked to citable publications or fuller protocol documentation.

## Citation and Metadata Notes

- The Longdom article uses a mistyped study ID (`NCT0360419`) inside the article text, while the actual registry record is `NCT03630419`.
- That mismatch should be preserved as a provenance note rather than silently corrected.

## Notes

- For final synthesis, keep a clear separation between `company-asserted`, `peer-reviewed`, and `registry` evidence.
- The two study notes worth promoting first are the enzyme paper and the MCI trial/article pair.
