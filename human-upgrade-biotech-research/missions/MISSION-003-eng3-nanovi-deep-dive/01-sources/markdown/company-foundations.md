## Company Facts

- Eng3 Corporation says it was founded in `2003` by engineer `Hans Eng`.
- The company frames NanoVi as a biophysics-based device family intended to support protein structure and folding rather than deliver a pharmaceutical or chemical intervention.
- Official pages position Eng3 as a medical-device company focused on oxidative stress, cell damage, protein folding, and repair support.
- The public-facing leadership page clearly names `Hans Eng` as founder and CEO and `Rowena Gates` as principal and vice president of business development.

## Origin Story and Leadership

- Hans Eng is presented as Berlin-born, trained as an engineer, and experienced in medical-device R&D, production, and quality assurance.
- Eng3 says Hans Eng previously worked at `Johnson & Johnson` in Europe and later founded and sold a German medical implant company before forming Eng3 in the United States.
- Rowena Gates is described as a Vancouver native, serial entrepreneur, former founder/CEO of `Aviarc Corporation`, and a longtime Eng3 business-development lead.
- The official leadership material reviewed in this mission did not surface a clearly structured scientific advisory board page. Treat any advisory or expert ecosystem claims as separate from named corporate leadership unless a dedicated official page is found.

## Business and Financial Context

- Eng3 behaves like a private company. No public revenue figures were found on the official site.
- An `edgar_search` lookup for `Eng3` returned no obvious SEC company match, which is consistent with the company appearing private and not obviously public-reporting.
- A third-party profile surfaced via search (`CB Insights`) and should be treated as secondary context rather than audited financial evidence.
- Official commercial materials emphasize referrals, international resellers, direct inquiry for pricing, and device sales rather than public capital-market activity.

## Operational Notes

- The public purchase page does not display retail prices and says this is due to agreements with international resellers.
- The referral-letter PDF indicates a structured sales/referral program with fixed referral payouts per device tier, reinforcing that Eng3 sells higher-ticket hardware rather than low-cost consumables.
