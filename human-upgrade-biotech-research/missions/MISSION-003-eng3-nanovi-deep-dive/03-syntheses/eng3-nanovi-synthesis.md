# Eng3 / NanoVi Mission Synthesis

## Bottom Line

Eng3 is a private Seattle-based device company founded in `2003` by `Hans Eng` and commercially built around the `NanoVi` platform. Its strongest evidence is not a large clinical program but a smaller, mixed-grade stack: one clearly peer-reviewed mechanistic paper on coherent humidity and enzyme activity, one tiny registered MCI/AD proof-of-concept study using diet plus cellular repair therapy, and a broader set of company-curated HRV, athletic, oxidative-stress, and practitioner case summaries that remain only partially externally resolved.

## Stage 1: Company Fundamentals

- `eng3corp.com` is the primary official domain and contains the key sections for company history, products, manuals, FAQs, and studies.
- `eng3.com` appears in official 2024 referral-program material and should be treated as a secondary official domain.
- Official leadership is clear at the executive layer: `Hans Eng` and `Rowena Gates`.
- Public financial disclosure is weak. No official revenue figure was found and no obvious SEC company record surfaced in this mission.

## Stage 2: Product and Device Findings

The core product line is a three-tier device stack:

1. [[nanovi-eco|NanoVi Eco]]
2. [[nanovi-pro|NanoVi Pro]]
3. [[nanovi-exo|NanoVi Exo]]

The main differences are output, session duration, smartcard capability, and commercial positioning. The underlying claim architecture is shared across all three devices:

- oxidative-stress repair support
- protein folding support
- bio-identical or coherent-humidity signaling
- wellness, recovery, and performance applications

The best spec-grade source is the technical-spec PDF rather than the marketing pages. Public retail prices are withheld, but the referral-letter PDF reveals clear internal device-tier economics.

## Stage 3: Studies, Trials, and Case Material

### Most Authoritative

- [[cohu-enzyme-activity-pmid-35054784|PMID 35054784]] is the strongest externally verified evidence object.
- [[nct03630419-mito-food-plan-cell-repair|NCT03630419]] is the strongest human-study object linked to the company’s claims.

### Important but Lower-Confidence

- HRV improvement in older adults
- HRV improvement in athletes
- reduced lactate
- stronger immune response
- stronger antioxidant defense
- reduced oxidative-stress markers after a session

These all appear in official Eng3 materials, but this mission did not resolve them to strong publication or registry records. They should therefore be framed as `company-asserted` or `company-summary` evidence unless stronger sourcing is found later.

## Evidence Quality Assessment

| Claim area | Assessment | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Coherent humidity / ordered-water mechanism | `Supported` mechanistically | Peer-reviewed in vitro evidence exists |
| Broad clinical efficacy across wellness, recovery, and performance | `Preliminary` | Human evidence is sparse, small, or company-mediated |
| MCI / AD improvement claim | `Preliminary` | Real registry record, but tiny and confounded mixed intervention |
| Athletic HRV / lactate / immune benefits | `Preliminary to speculative` | Company summaries dominate; external validation incomplete |

## Practical Read

NanoVi looks less like a mature evidence-backed medical program and more like a premium hardware platform with an interesting mechanistic paper, a handful of early human signals, and a much larger outer ring of marketing-amplified summaries and case stories.
