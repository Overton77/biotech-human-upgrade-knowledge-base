# TruDiagnostic company fundamentals and business context

## Working Assessment

TruDiagnostic currently presents as a Lexington, Kentucky-based epigenetic testing company spanning consumer testing, provider-facing diagnostics, research services, and commercial partnerships. The strongest official evidence supports the brand, operating location, business-line structure, CLIA-lab positioning, and active research-commercialization posture. Exact legal-entity confirmation remains incomplete.

## Strongest Company-Context Findings

- TruDiagnostic publicly frames itself as an epigenetic diagnostics company rather than just a single-product longevity test.
- The website architecture supports a multi-line business model, not a simple DTC storefront.
- Official pages emphasize research infrastructure, bioinformatics, custom-array work, and multi-omic integration.
- Official pages and distributed press materials indicate the company is still actively seeking research and commercial partnerships.
- The 2025 NIH SBIR announcement suggests a meaningful R&D narrative beyond ordinary consumer-test marketing.

## Contradictions And Cautions

- Public self-reported scale metrics are not internally consistent across official pages.
- The `team` page cites `15,000+` tested patients, while other current company materials describe `100,000+`.
- Leadership normalization is incomplete because the current extract exposes some role holders only by first name.
- I did not confirm a clean SEC or Form D trail for TruDiagnostic in this pass.

## Implications For Later Mission Stages

- Stage 2 should treat TruDiagnostic as a product ecosystem under a broader diagnostics and research platform.
- Browser-assisted work will likely be needed for `shop.trudiagnostic.com` and help-center or support surfaces.
- Stage 3 evidence review should explicitly separate:
  - company-built algorithms and reports
  - company-linked publications
  - externally validated literature
  - registry or filing-grade evidence
