# TruDiagnostic support-center articles and sample TruAge report

## Summary

This gap-fill source set substantially improves the public evidence surface for Stage 2 and Stage 4. It adds:

- a public sample TruAge PDF
- support-center articles clarifying `TruAge` versus `TruHealth`
- support-center language about the biomarker logic of the TruAge test

## Key Findings

### Product distinction

The support-center distinction is clearer than the main marketing pages:

- `TruAge` is framed as the biological-aging dashboard
- `TruHealth` is framed as the systems-health and precision-nutrition layer using epigenetic biomarker proxies

### Sample-report details now visible

The public sample TruAge PDF exposes:

- `OMICmAge`
- `SYMPHONYAge`
- `DunedinPACE`
- `Telomere Length`
- `Inflammation` with CRP and IL-6
- `Immune Health`
- `OMICm Fit Age`
- smoking and alcohol risk outputs
- a large `Additional Epigenetic Biomarkers` section

### Organ-system training biomarkers exposed in the PDF

The sample report also exposes system-specific biomarker-training lists for `SYMPHONYAge`, including:

- blood markers such as ferritin, hematocrit, hemoglobin, platelet indices, and red-cell indices
- brain markers such as homocysteine, BDNF, clusterin, stroke history, and cognition scores
- inflammation markers such as ferritin, CRP, TGF-beta, IL-10, IL-1 receptor antagonist, IL-6, and TNF receptor 1
- hormone markers such as IGF-1 and DHEAS
- kidney markers such as albumin, urea nitrogen, chloride, bicarbonate, creatinine, cystatin C, potassium, and sodium
- liver markers such as albumin, alkaline phosphatase, ALT, AST, bilirubin, and total protein
- metabolic markers such as fasting glucose, HDL-C, LDL-C, triglycerides, BMI, waist circumference, CRP, and IL-6
- lung markers such as peak expiratory flow, bicarbonate, chronic lung disease, wheezing, phlegm, and smoking-pack-years
- musculoskeletal markers such as vitamin D3, DHEAS, IGF-1, arthritis, BMI, grip strength, timed walk, and balance tests

### Retest guidance strengthened

The sample report explicitly says to retest every `3 months` to track progress for the pace-of-aging layer.
