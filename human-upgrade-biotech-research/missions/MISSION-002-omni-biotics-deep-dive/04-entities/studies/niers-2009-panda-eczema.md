# The PandA Study

## Study Overview

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| PMID | `19392993` |
| Design | Double-blind randomized placebo-controlled trial |
| Population | High-risk mothers and offspring |
| Intervention | Prenatal and postnatal probiotic supplementation |
| Main outcome | Eczema prevention in early life |

## Main findings

- Lower parental-reported eczema in the intervention group during the first 3 months of life.
- Preventive signal appeared strongest early and persisted directionally over follow-up.
- Increased colonization with `Lc. lactis` and lowered IL-5 at 3 months.

## Why it matters

- This is the flagship evidence anchor for [[omni-biotic-panda]].
- It gives the mission one of its clearest product-to-trial relationships.

## Caveats

- The strongest effect was early rather than uniformly large across all later timepoints.
- As always, formulation matching should be preserved carefully, but this is still one of the mission’s strongest product-linked studies.
