# Multispecies Synbiotic in Diabesity

## Study Overview

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| PMID | `31729622` |
| Design | Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled pilot study |
| Population | `26` patients with diabesity |
| Duration | `6 months` |
| Main outcomes | Glucose metabolism, gut permeability, microbiome, quality of life |

## Main findings

- No significant change in the primary glucose-metabolism outcome.
- Improvements were reported in zonulin, lipoprotein(a), hip circumference, and one quality-of-life domain.
- The study supports a modest adjunctive metabolic signal rather than a dramatic glycemic effect.

## Why it matters

- Strong PubMed-backed metabolic / gut-permeability evidence line linked by the company to the Hetox family.

## Caveats

- Pilot study with limited sample size.
- Formulation equivalence to the current U.S. Hetox product should be stated carefully rather than assumed absolutely.
