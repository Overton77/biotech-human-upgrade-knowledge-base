# Probiotic Supplementation in Trained Athletes

## Study Overview

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| PMID | `27886064` |
| Design | Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial |
| Population | `33` highly trained athletes |
| Duration | `12 weeks` |
| Main outcomes | URTI incidence, tryptophan-kynurenine metabolism, exercise-related biomarker changes |

## Main findings

- Lower incidence of upper respiratory tract infection symptoms in the probiotic group.
- Reduced exercise-induced tryptophan degradation signal versus placebo.
- Did not demonstrate athletic-performance improvement per se.

## Why it matters

- Best flagship study for [[omni-biotic-power]].
- Strongly aligned with the product’s athlete / resilience / oxidative-stress positioning.

## Caveats

- Supports immune and biochemical resilience more than direct performance enhancement.
- Still a specialized athletic population.
