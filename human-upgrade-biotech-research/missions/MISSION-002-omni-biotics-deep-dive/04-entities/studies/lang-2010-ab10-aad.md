# Use of a multi-species probiotic for the prevention of antibiotic associated diarrhea

This is one of the central AB 10 evidence references repeatedly cited by the OMNi-BiOTiC site.

## Why it matters

- It is one of the main public references used to support the AB 10 restoration narrative.
- It connects directly to the antibiotic-associated diarrhea use case.
- The company uses it in both product-page and research-hub contexts.

## Study Read

- Company materials describe this as an observational study in post-surgical patients.
- The core reported takeaway is reduced antibiotic-associated diarrhea and lower healthcare costs.
- In the current mission, this is still a lower-visibility evidence object than the PubMed-backed Stress Release, Panda, Power, Hetox, and Caricol lines.

## Core takeaways

- Significant reduction in antibiotic-associated diarrhea
- Reduction in overall healthcare costs

## Caveats

- The exact 2010 article is a company-linked citation rather than one of the strongest PubMed-anchored records in this mission.
- It remains useful, but should be labeled more cautiously than the clearer PubMed-indexed OMNi-BiOTiC trials.
