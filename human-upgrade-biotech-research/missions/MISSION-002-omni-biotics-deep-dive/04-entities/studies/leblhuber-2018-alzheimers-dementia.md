# Probiotic Supplementation in Alzheimer's Dementia

## Study Overview

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| PMID | `30101706` |
| Population | `20` outpatients with Alzheimer's disease |
| Duration | `4 weeks` |
| Main outcomes | Gut inflammation markers, microbiota composition, tryptophan metabolism, immune activation |

## Main findings

- Decline in fecal zonulin after supplementation.
- Increase in `Faecalibacterium prausnitzii`.
- Signals in tryptophan-kynurenine metabolism and immune activation markers.

## Why it matters

- Important exploratory human study for the Stress Release / Stress Repair gut-brain evidence stack.
- Gives the mission a dementia-relevant literature link in addition to the later `PIDE` registry.

## Caveats

- Exploratory design with small sample size.
- Biomarker-oriented more than clinically transformative.
