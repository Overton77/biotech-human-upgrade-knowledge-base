# Effects of an Oral Synbiotic in IBS-D Patients

## Study Overview

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| PMID | `30251020` |
| Product term in paper | `OMNi-BiOTiC Stress Repair` |
| Population | `10` IBS-D patients |
| Duration | `4 weeks` |
| Main outcomes | Mucosal/fecal microbiota, immune cells, SCFAs, zonulin, IBS severity |

## Main findings

- Increased microbial diversity in gastric and duodenal mucosal samples.
- Reduced fecal zonulin and IBS severity score.
- Increased acetate and butyrate in stool after intervention.

## Why it matters

- One of the more disease-relevant clinical studies tied to the Stress Release / Stress Repair formulation family.
- Helps bridge the gap between healthy-volunteer gut-brain studies and symptomatic clinical populations.

## Caveats

- Very small sample size.
- Short intervention window.
