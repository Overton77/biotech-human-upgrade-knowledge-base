# Probiotics drive gut microbiome triggering emotional brain signatures

This is one of the clearest company-linked human studies for the OMNi-BiOTiC Stress Release evidence stack.

## Why it matters

- It is a randomized controlled trial.
- It is directly tied to the gut-brain axis narrative used for Stress Release.
- It links microbiome changes with measurable emotional / cognitive signatures.

## Study Overview

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| PMID | `29723105` |
| DOI | `10.1080/19490976.2018.1460015` |
| Journal | `Gut Microbes` |
| Design | Double-blind, placebo-controlled randomized trial |
| Population | `45` healthy volunteers split across probiotic, placebo, and control groups |
| Intervention window | `4 weeks` |

## Core takeaways

- Improved positive affect and cognitive reactivity
- Improved memory performance
- Improved gut microbiome diversity

## Caveats

- Healthy-volunteer study rather than a disease-treatment trial.
- Strong for gut-brain plausibility, but not itself proof of psychiatric or medical efficacy in patients.
