# OMNi-BiOTiC Mission Synthesis

## Bottom Line

OMNi-BiOTiC is a private, family-led microbiome company centered on Institut AllergoSan in Graz, Austria, with a U.S. retail/distribution layer that sells a broad catalog of probiotics, prebiotics, digestive aids, pet products, and bundles. The company’s strongest public-facing evidence comes from product-specific, often final-formulation studies organized around gut-brain, gut-antibiotic, gut-liver, maternal/neonatal, immune, and athletic-performance themes.

## Company Picture

- Institut AllergoSan was founded in 1991 and presents itself as a microbiome research and natural-medicine R&D company.
- The company emphasizes a family leadership model and long-term scientific collaboration.
- Official pages point to around 40-country reach and a market-leader position in Austria/Germany.
- Revenue is not publicly disclosed on the official sites, so directory estimates should be labeled as such.

See `[[company-foundations]]`.

## Product Architecture

The live U.S. catalog clusters into four practical buckets:

1. Multi-strain probiotics
2. Prebiotics
3. Digestive aids
4. Bundles / programs that sequence existing formulas

The catalog is not just "a probiotic company"; it is a portfolio of:

- stress / gut-brain formulas
- antibiotic restoration formulas
- immune / allergy-related formulas
- gut-liver and cardiometabolic formulas
- maternal/neonatal formulas
- athletic-performance formulas
- prebiotic fiber support
- papaya-based digestive aids

See `[[product-catalog]]`.

## Evidence Quality Assessment

### Stronger Evidence Areas

- `Stress Release` has multiple company-cited human studies, including gut-brain and gut connectivity work.
- `AB 10` has a relatively deep evidence trail around antibiotic-associated diarrhea and microbiome restoration.
- `Hetox` is backed by gut-liver / cardiometabolic studies with human trial language.
- `Panda` has a recognizable prenatal / infant / eczema evidence thread.
- `Power` has athletic-performance and oxidative-stress studies.
- `Caricol` and `Caricol Gastro` have clear digestive / gastritis evidence threads.

### More Carefully Framed Areas

- `Balance` looks more like a legacy immune / GALT formulation with older evidence and rationale.
- Bundles should be treated as curated combinations rather than new formulas.
- Company citations frequently mix study results, product marketing, and educational claims in the same page.

### Caveats

- The research pages are company-controlled.
- Some study summaries are company descriptions of published work rather than the publication text itself.
- Third-party operational metrics are directory estimates, not audited figures.

See `[[research-hub]]`.

## Trial / Registry Signal

Search results surfaced multiple relevant registrations, suggesting the broader brand ecosystem extends beyond the current U.S. retail lineup:

- vaginal microbiome and bacterial vaginosis studies
- endometriosis / fertility studies
- kidney transplantation
- colorectal cancer adjunctive probiotics

That means the research program is broader than the retail catalog and should be tracked separately when appropriate.
