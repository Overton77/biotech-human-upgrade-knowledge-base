# A Clinically Validated Human Saliva Metatranscriptomic Test for Global Systems Biology Studies

## Study Overview

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| PMID | `36622006` |
| Design | methods and clinical-validation paper |
| Sample type | `<100 ul` saliva |
| Main contribution | automated ambient-temperature saliva metatranscriptomics |

## Why it matters

- This is one of the most direct published anchors for Viome's saliva-testing platform.
- It supports public claims around safe shipping, preserved RNA, and scalable saliva metatranscriptomics.
- It underpins both oral-health and diagnostics-oriented parts of the Viome stack.

## Main findings

- The paper reports a high-throughput saliva metatranscriptomic test using less than `100 ul` of saliva.
- Sample preservation was reported to support storage and transport for up to `28 days` at ambient temperature.
- The workflow was framed as suitable for longitudinal and global-scale systems-biology research.

## Viome linkage

- Authors and affiliations include `Viome Life Sciences` and related Viome entities.
- Grant information includes `Viome Life Sciences`.
- This is a strong company-linked methods paper and one of the core published supports for Viome's saliva platform.

## Caveats

- Like the blood-transcriptome paper, this validates workflow and collection performance more than downstream consumer-score clinical utility.
- It should support platform-method claims, not be overextended into proof of all product recommendations.
