# Detecting Salivary Host and Microbiome RNA Signature for Aiding Diagnosis of Oral and Throat Cancer

## Study Overview

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| PMID | `37454545` |
| Design | training plus independent validation diagnostic study |
| Population | `1,175` high-risk participants overall; independent validation cohort of `230` |
| Disease focus | OSCC and OPSCC |

## Why it matters

- This is the single most important peer-reviewed paper for Viome's `CancerDetect` / `CDOT` program in the current mission.
- It directly evaluates a saliva-based host plus microbial metatranscriptomic signature for oral and throat cancer detection.
- It is the best bridge between Viome's public marketing claims and a published diagnostic-validation object.

## Main findings

- The classifier was trained on `945` participants and prospectively validated on `230` unseen samples.
- In the validation cohort, specificity was reported at `94%`.
- Sensitivity was reported at `90%` for `OSCC` and `84.2%` for `OPSCC`.
- Similar classification performance was reported across early-stage and later-stage cases.

## Viome linkage

- Viome Research Institute and Viome Life Sciences authors are directly represented, including Guruduth Banavar.
- The paper explicitly describes development and validation of `CancerDetect for Oral & Throat cancer (CDOT)`.
- This is company-linked but still a materially important evidence object because it is peer reviewed and directly on-point.

## Caveats

- The study is still company-linked and should not be treated as independent replication.
- It supports a promising diagnostic-aid program, not a population-standard screening recommendation.
- Public website performance language should be checked against the paper's exact reported values rather than paraphrased from marketing copy.
