# A Clinically Validated Human Capillary Blood Transcriptome Test for Global Systems Biology Studies

## Study Overview

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| PMID | `32772558` |
| Design | methods and clinical-validation paper |
| Sample type | `50 ul` finger-prick capillary blood |
| Main contribution | ambient-temperature whole-blood transcriptome workflow for population-scale studies |

## Why it matters

- This is one of the clearest papers showing how Viome's platform extends beyond stool and saliva into blood-based transcriptomics.
- It supports the logistics and lab-operations story behind Viome's broader systems-biology claims.
- It is especially relevant to [[full-body-intelligence]] and Viome's multi-sample testing story.

## Main findings

- The paper describes a capillary-blood transcriptome test using a very small finger-prick sample.
- Samples were reported as stable at ambient temperature for up to `4 weeks`.
- The workflow was designed for automated clinical-lab and bioinformatic processing to reduce batch effects.

## Viome linkage

- Viome authors are on the paper, including Ryan Toma and other Viome-affiliated contributors.
- Affiliations include `Viome, Inc.` in Bellevue together with related Viome-linked locations.
- This is best interpreted as a company-linked platform-method paper, not an independent clinical-utility outcome trial.

## Caveats

- The paper supports technical feasibility and workflow robustness, not direct disease-specific clinical utility.
- It is most useful as a methods anchor for Viome's multi-omic / systems-biology platform claims.
