# PMID 41174221

## Summary

Randomized placebo-controlled Nature Aging study showing that 4 weeks of 1000 mg/day Urolithin A in healthy middle-aged adults altered T-cell composition and immune-metabolic markers in ways consistent with reduced age-related immune decline.

## Key Details

- Population: `50` healthy middle-aged adults
- Dose: `1000 mg/day`
- Duration: `4 weeks`
- Registry: `NCT05735886`
- Main outcomes:
  - peripheral T-cell subset changes
  - immune metabolic remodeling
  - inflammaging-related signals

## Interpretation

- Stronger support for immune-aging claims than older Timeline press materials had.
- Still commercially linked:
  - Anurag Singh and Amazentis affiliations are present in the paper trail.
- Supports a narrower statement such as `immune-cell remodeling in a short-term randomized trial` better than a broad claim of generalized immune rejuvenation.

## Notes

- PubMed also surfaces an author correction in 2026.
- The existence of a correction does not negate the trial, but it is worth preserving for provenance.
