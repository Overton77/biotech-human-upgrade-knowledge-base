# Timeline Publications And Trial Source Hub

## Official Study Page Signals

- The current `timeline.com/studies` page presents Mitopure as the centerpiece of the company's evidence narrative.
- It lists clinical, preclinical, and review-style publications together on one page, so the study page is useful for discovery but not sufficient by itself for evidence grading.
- The strongest directly traceable human-study anchors surfaced in this mission are:
  - Nature Metabolism 2019 first-in-human study
  - JAMA Network Open 2022 older-adult randomized clinical trial
  - Cell Reports Medicine 2022 middle-aged randomized clinical trial
  - Nature Aging 2025 immune-aging randomized placebo-controlled trial

## Key Human Evidence Anchors

### First-in-human biomarker / safety study

- Title: `The mitophagy activator urolithin A is safe and induces a molecular signature of improved mitochondrial and cellular health in humans`
- Journal: `Nature Metabolism` (2019)
- PMID: `32694802`
- DOI: `10.1038/s42255-019-0073-4`
- Key signal:
  - 4-week dosing in healthy sedentary older adults
  - safety and bioavailability emphasized
  - biomarker and gene-expression changes, not a hard clinical-outcome trial

### Older-adult muscle-endurance trial

- Title: `Effect of Urolithin A Supplementation on Muscle Endurance and Mitochondrial Health in Older Adults: A Randomized Clinical Trial`
- Journal: `JAMA Network Open` (2022)
- PMID: `35050355`
- DOI: `10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2021.44279`
- Registry link surfaced in search trail: `NCT03283462` was referenced in review coverage, though this mission pass captured the publication more cleanly than the registry record itself
- Key signal:
  - 66 older adults
  - 1000 mg daily for 4 months
  - improved muscle-endurance measures and favorable biomarker shifts
  - primary whole-body outcomes were more mixed than marketing headlines imply

### Middle-aged muscle / performance trial

- Title: `Urolithin A improves muscle strength, exercise performance, and biomarkers of mitochondrial health in a randomized trial in middle-aged adults`
- Journal: `Cell Reports Medicine` (2022)
- PMID: `35584623`
- DOI: `10.1016/j.xcrm.2022.100633`
- Registry: `NCT03464500`
- Key signal:
  - overweight, low-endurance adults aged roughly 40 to 65
  - 500 mg and 1000 mg arms over 4 months
  - muscle strength and several biomarker improvements
  - peak power output primary endpoint was not significant

### Immune-aging trial

- Title: `Effect of the mitophagy inducer urolithin A on age-related immune decline: a randomized, placebo-controlled trial`
- Journal: `Nature Aging` (2025)
- PMID: `41174221`
- DOI: `10.1038/s43587-025-00996-x`
- Registry: `NCT05735886`
- Key signal:
  - 50 healthy middle-aged adults
  - 1000 mg daily for 4 weeks
  - primary outcomes centered on T-cell composition and immune metabolic remodeling
  - this is stronger than press-release-only evidence, but still company-linked through authorship and affiliations

## Trial Registry Signals

- `NCT02655393`: single and multiple dose AMAZ-02 / Urolithin A first-in-human study
- `NCT04783207`: muscle-function / iron / performance study involving Mitopure in trained runners
- `NCT05735886`: immune-aging clinical trial that aligns with the Nature Aging paper
- `NCT07231848`: bioavailability of four Mitopure formulations
- `NCT07231783`: confirmatory muscle-function study with 500 mg / 1000 mg / placebo arms
- Additional registry hits surfaced in the raw search trail, including brain-longevity and CSF-kinetics studies, but those were not normalized in depth in this mission pass

## Skin-Evidence Signals

- Timeline points to a topical Urolithin A / Mitopure skin-aging paper and uses it heavily in skincare marketing.
- The current source trail most clearly identifies:
  - medRxiv preprint on topical application of Urolithin A slowing intrinsic skin aging and protecting from UVB-mediated photodamage
- This should be treated as weaker than the peer-reviewed oral studies unless a final journal publication is confirmed.

## Evidence Cautions

- Timeline's study page blends:
  - direct company studies
  - preclinical studies
  - review articles
  - mechanistic support
- Several papers include `Amazentis` / `Timeline` authors or collaborators, so sponsorship and commercial linkage must be tracked explicitly.
- Marketing language often compresses biomarker, functional, and mechanistic outcomes into a broader longevity narrative.
