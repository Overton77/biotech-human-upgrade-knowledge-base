# Timeline Product And Skincare Matrix

## Core Commercial Pattern

Timeline's active catalog is narrower and cleaner than many longevity brands. The main oral business is currently built around three formats delivering the same core active dose of Mitopure, while the skincare business extends the same branded-ingredient logic into a six-product topical line.

### Interpretation

- The oral line is essentially a delivery-format strategy rather than a multi-ingredient stack strategy.
- This reduces formulation complexity and keeps Mitopure itself as the hero.
- The product pages repeatedly encourage 4-month adherence and sometimes discuss 1000 mg/day usage, aligning product guidance with the more ambitious study narrative.

## Oral Formulation Read

- Unlike many performance / longevity products, these oral SKUs do not rely on broad proprietary blends.
- Each page foregrounds one active:
  - `Mitopure (Urolithin A)`
- Most differentiation comes from excipient and format choices:
  - gummies: fiber, pectin, flavoring, colorants, Reb M
  - softgels: lipid vehicle and capsule system
  - powder: berry powders, pomegranate extract, rice fiber, flavor system

## Skincare Ingredient Read

- The strongest ingredient transparency in this mission pass came from the `Firming Serum`.
- The firming serum combines Mitopure with conventional topical actives:
  - niacinamide
  - peptide complex
  - panthenol
  - sodium hyaluronate
  - phytic acid
  - additional hydration and barrier-support excipients
- The skincare ingredients page suggests Mitopure is reused broadly across the skincare line, but product-specific full INCI coverage for all six products remains incomplete.

## Strategic Read

Timeline is commercializing `Mitopure` in two ways at once:

1. as a branded active delivered directly in oral supplement formats
2. as a longevity-science platform ingredient embedded into premium skincare

This creates a strong, platform-style brand architecture:

- same ingredient story
- multiple consumer rituals
- nutrition plus beauty positioning

## Commercial Risks / Gaps

- Oral catalog completeness is strong.
- Skincare catalog completeness is good at the product-list level, but only partial at the full-ingredient-list level.
- The main missing commercial detail from the current pass is a full, product-by-product INCI normalization across the entire skincare line.
