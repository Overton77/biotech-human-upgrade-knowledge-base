# Timeline Mission Synthesis

## Summary

Timeline currently operates as a Swiss-born longevity brand built around `Mitopure`, its proprietary Urolithin A platform, while first-party materials still identify `Amazentis` as the parent scientific organization behind the program. The active commercial system is coherent: three oral products delivering the same 500 mg Mitopure daily dose in different formats, plus an expanding six-product skincare line that extends the Mitopure platform into beauty.

The strongest public evidence base is in oral use cases tied to mitochondrial health, muscle function, and related biomarkers. Timeline's evidence quality is higher than that of many supplement brands, but much of the strongest human literature remains company-linked. The immune-aging story is materially strengthened by the 2025 Nature Aging trial, while the skincare evidence is still less mature than the oral-muscle literature.

## Key Findings

- Timeline's current public materials clearly present `Timeline` as the commercialization brand and `Amazentis` as the parent / originating science company.
- The oral product catalog is concentrated around three active formats:
  - gummies
  - softgels
  - powder
- Each main oral product currently sells for `$99` one-time with lower subscription pricing at longer durations.
- The browser inspection confirmed that the current oral product pages expose full ingredient panels in text, not just images.
- The active skincare line currently includes at least:
  - firming serum
  - eye cream
  - gentle cleanser
  - resurfacing exfoliator
  - dewy cream
  - barrier cream
- The strongest human evidence anchors captured in this mission are:
  - first-in-human biomarker / safety study: PMID `32694802`
  - older-adult muscle-endurance RCT: PMID `35050355`
  - middle-aged muscle-strength / performance RCT: PMID `35584623`
  - immune-aging RCT: PMID `41174221`
- Timeline's marketing is often directionally aligned with the evidence, but the highest-confidence support is still narrower than the broadest longevity framing used on the site.

- Has the topical Urolithin A skin-aging preprint advanced to peer-reviewed publication?
- What are the full INCI lists for the non-serum skincare products currently active in the catalog?
