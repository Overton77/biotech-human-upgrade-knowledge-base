# STEMTech International

## Identity

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Legal names | STEMTech International, Inc. / Stemtech HealthSciences, Inc. / Stemtech Corporation |
| OTC ticker | STEK (historical) |
| Distribution model | **Multi-level marketing (MLM)** |
| Status | **Defunct** — filed Chapter 11 bankruptcy February 2017 |
| Key product | **StemEnhance** (AFA-based stem cell mobilizer) |

## History

- **~2005:** Co-founded by [[christian-drapeau|Christian Drapeau]], Ray Carter, and Greg Newman
- **2005–2016:** Operated as MLM selling StemEnhance, StemFlo, ST-5 with MigraStem
- **2015:** California Prop 65 settlement — products contained lead without warnings ($118K total)
- **2016 (March):** Drapeau resigns from board of directors
- **2016 (July):** STEMTech sues Drapeau (Case 1:16-cv-00918, W.D. Texas) — breach of contract, trade secret misappropriation
- **2017 (February):** STEMTech files Chapter 11 bankruptcy
- **2018 (November):** Lawsuit against Drapeau terminated — court found STEMTech failed to establish trade secret claims

## Significance

STEMTech is the **predecessor company** to [[stemregen-kalyagen|STEMREGEN / Kalyagen]]. The early peer-reviewed research on AFA stem cell mobilization (PMID 17765649, 20404540) was conducted under STEMTech affiliation. The company's MLM structure is important context for understanding the commercial origins of the endogenous stem cell mobilization supplement category.
