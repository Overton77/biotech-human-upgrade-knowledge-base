# STEMREGEN Compound Analysis

Pharmacological assessment of key active compounds across the [[stemregen-kalyagen|STEMREGEN]] product line.

## Compounds with Legitimate Stem Cell Mobilization Evidence (with caveats)

### 1. Fucoidan (from Fucus vesiculosus, Ascophyllum nodosum)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Present in | [[stemregen-release|Release]], [[stemregen-mobilize|Mobilize]] |
| Mechanism | L-selectin binding, SDF-1 displacement from heparan sulfate |
| Evidence | IV/IP studies in animals — proven HSPC mobilizer (*Blood*, *PNAS*) |
| **Critical gap** | **Oral bioavailability is extremely low** — large sulfated polysaccharide poorly absorbed from GI tract |
| Assessment | Strong parenteral evidence; oral supplementation claim is a major extrapolation |

### 2. Beta-1,3-Glucan

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Present in | [[stemregen-release|Release]], [[stemregen-sport|Release SPORT]] |
| Mechanism | Complement activation, MMP-9 release, "trained immunity" of BM progenitors |
| Evidence | Independent *Cell* papers on trained immunity; IV/IP mobilization in mice |
| **Critical distinction** | BM progenitor reprogramming ≠ mobilization into circulation |
| Assessment | Legitimate immunology; mechanism differs from STEMREGEN's mobilization narrative |

### 3. Ginsenoside Rg1 (Panax notoginseng)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Present in | [[stemregen-release|Release]], [[stemregen-sport|Release SPORT]] |
| Mechanism | SDF-1/CXCR4 modulation, Wnt/β-catenin, Notch signaling |
| Evidence | Strongest independent preclinical evidence for stem cell regulation |
| **Critical gap** | Oral bioavailability only ~5–10% |
| Assessment | Promising preclinical; oral translation uncertain |

## Compounds with Strong Pharmacology but No Stem Cell Mobilization Evidence

### 4. Phycocyanin (from AFA / Spirulina)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Present in | [[stemregen-signal|Signal]] (Spirulina extract), indirectly in StemAFA |
| Mechanism | COX-2 inhibition, NF-κB modulation, antioxidant |
| Note | The AFA mobilization claim comes from **whole AFA extract** (L-selectin ligands), not phycocyanin itself |
| Assessment | Excellent anti-inflammatory; mobilization attribution is misplaced |

### 5. Pterostilbene

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Present in | [[stemregen-sport|Release SPORT]], [[stemregen-signal|Signal]] |
| Mechanism | Sirtuin activation, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory |
| Bioavailability | ~80% (excellent for a polyphenol) |
| Stem cell literature | Primarily about **suppressing cancer stem cells**, not mobilizing healthy ones |
| Assessment | Good antioxidant; stem cell mobilization claim unsupported |

### 6. Hydroxytyrosol (olive extract)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Present in | [[stemregen-mobilize|Mobilize]] |
| Mechanism | Potent antioxidant, endothelial protection, Nrf2 activation |
| Regulatory | EFSA-authorized health claim for cardiovascular protection |
| Stem cell evidence | One animal neurogenesis study; no mobilization data |
| Assessment | Well-validated antioxidant; no stem cell mobilization evidence |

### 7. Nattokinase

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Present in | [[stemregen-mobilize|Mobilize]] |
| Mechanism | Serine protease — fibrinolytic (dissolves fibrin clots) |
| Evidence | Proven fibrinolytic in human studies |
| Role | Included for microcirculation support, not stem cell mobilization |
| Assessment | Legitimate use for blood fluidity; correctly positioned |

### 8. Astaxanthin

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Present in | [[stemregen-signal|Signal]] |
| Mechanism | Carotenoid antioxidant (quenches singlet oxygen, peroxyl radicals) |
| Evidence | Extensive literature on antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, cardioprotective effects |
| Stem cell evidence | Protects stem cells from oxidative damage in vitro |
| Assessment | Well-validated antioxidant; indirect stem cell support at best |

## Summary Assessment

| Category | Compounds | Verdict |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| Legitimate mobilization evidence (parenteral) | Fucoidan, beta-glucan | Proven when injected; oral translation is the gap |
| Promising preclinical | Ginsenoside Rg1 | Best independent evidence; bioavailability concern |
| Strong pharmacology, no mobilization evidence | Phycocyanin, pterostilbene, hydroxytyrosol, astaxanthin | Good compounds, wrong claim |
| Correctly positioned | Nattokinase, NAC, L-citrulline, beetroot | Legitimate for their stated roles (circulation, antioxidant) |
| Insufficient evidence | StemAloe (Aloe macroclada) | Published only in predatory journal |
