## Protocol Details

### Study Design

The trial is registered as **OBSERVATIONAL** but the summary text describes it as a **"prospective, randomized, controlled trial"** — this is a significant discrepancy. The study design classification is internally inconsistent.

- **Allocation:** Not explicitly stated (arms suggest randomization)
- **Masking:** Not described (likely open-label given the SVF surgical procedure)
- **Follow-up:** 6 months (primary), 12 months (secondary)

### Arms / Groups (3 arms, N=15 total → ~5 per arm)

| Arm | Intervention | Description |
|-----|-------------|-------------|
| **Arm 1: Conventional + AFA** | Dietary supplement | Conventional heart failure therapy + AFA extract capsules (2 capsules × 3 times/day) for 6 months |
| **Arm 2: Conventional + SVF** | Surgical procedure | Conventional therapy + autologous adipose stromal vascular fraction (SVF) injection |
| **Arm 3: Conventional + SVF + AFA** | Combined | Conventional therapy + SVF injection + AFA capsules for 6 months |

### Interventions Detail

**AFA (Aphanizomenon flos-aquae) Extract:**
- Form: Oral capsules
- Dosing: 2 capsules, 3 times per day
- Duration: 6 months
- Route: Oral
- Classification: Dietary supplement
- Note: The specific product/brand is NOT named in the registration. No mention of "STEMREGEN" or "Kalyagen" in the trial record.

**SVF (Stromal Vascular Fraction):**
- Source: Autologous adipose tissue
- Procedure: Mini-liposuction under local anesthesia with Klein solution
- Volume: ~70 cc dry fat tissue
- Processing: Collagenase digestion + centrifugation
- Route: Not specified (likely intracardiac or intracoronary — not stated)
- Note: The route of SVF administration is critically absent from the protocol

### Primary Outcome Measures

1. **Change in NYHA functional class** — assessed with SAQ-7 questionnaire at 6 months
2. **Change in echocardiographic parameters** — at 0 and 6 months

### Secondary Outcome Measures

1. **Change in NYHA functional class** — SAQ-7 at 12 months
2. **Change in echocardiographic parameters** — at 0 and 12 months

### Eligibility Criteria

**Inclusion:**
- Ejection Fraction (EF) < 45%
- NYHA Class II–III
- Age: 35–80 years
- Both sexes

**Exclusion:**
- Acute coronary syndrome within 6 weeks
- Pregnancy
- FEV1 < 20%
- Cancer
- Severe comorbid disease
- Immunodeficiency
- Investigator discretion

### Independence Assessment

**RED FLAGS:**

1. **Ghost sponsor:** The "Sociedad Española de Medicina Regenerativa y Terapia Celular" has zero PubMed publications and only 2 ClinicalTrials.gov registrations — both testing the same class of intervention (AFA/stem cell mobilizers).

2. **STEMREGEN explicitly named in the Parkinson's trial (NCT05699694):** The sister trial from the same sponsor, same investigators, same clinic explicitly names "StemRegen product" as the intervention — 6 capsules/day for 6 months. This strongly suggests the AFA extract in NCT04515537 is also a Kalyagen/STEMREGEN product, even though it is not named as such.

3. **Study design misclassification:** Registered as "observational" but described as "randomized controlled trial" — this may reflect unfamiliarity with ClinicalTrials.gov registration requirements or an attempt to avoid the stricter regulatory requirements for interventional trials.

4. **Tiny sample size:** N=15 across 3 arms = ~5 patients per arm. This is statistically underpowered for any meaningful efficacy conclusion in heart failure.

5. **Gmail contact:** The lead investigator uses a personal Gmail address rather than an institutional email.

6. **No institutional affiliation:** The clinic "Castelló 68" appears to be a private practice, not a university hospital or research institution.

## Connection to STEMREGEN / Kalyagen

### Direct Evidence

The **sister trial NCT05699694** (Parkinson's Disease) from the same sponsor, same investigators, same clinic **explicitly names "StemRegen product"** as the intervention:

> *"Products will be consumed orally in capsules with a glass of water. Six capsules per day will be consumed daily for 6 months."*

The Parkinson's trial description states:
> *"Suggested use of the food supplement actually sold in the USA and in Europe"*

This language directly mirrors Kalyagen's commercial product marketing.

### Indirect Evidence

- The AFA extract dosing in NCT04515537 (2 capsules × 3 times/day = 6 capsules/day) matches the STEMREGEN product dosing in NCT05699694 (6 capsules/day)
- Christian Drapeau (Kalyagen founder) is the primary researcher behind AFA stem cell mobilization science (PMIDs: 17765649, 20404540, 19331184)
- The entire scientific premise of using AFA for stem cell mobilization originates from Drapeau's work, first at STEMTech HealthSciences, then at Biomx Stemceuticals, now at Kalyagen

### Assessment

**High confidence that NCT04515537 is testing a Kalyagen/STEMREGEN product**, even though the brand name is not disclosed in the heart failure trial registration. The same investigators, sponsor, clinic, dosing regimen, and scientific premise link both trials directly to Kalyagen's commercial product line.

## Clinical Significance Assessment

### Strengths
- Addresses an interesting hypothesis (botanical stem cell mobilization in CHF)
- Includes a combination arm (SVF + AFA) that could test synergy
- Uses objective endpoints (echocardiographic parameters)

### Critical Weaknesses

1. **Grossly underpowered:** N=15 across 3 arms (~5/arm) cannot detect clinically meaningful differences in ejection fraction or NYHA class. Standard heart failure trials enroll hundreds to thousands.

2. **Design misclassification:** Listed as observational but described as randomized — raises questions about regulatory oversight and ethical review.

3. **Missing protocol details:** No description of SVF delivery route (intracardiac? intracoronary? intramyocardial?), no blinding, no randomization method, no power calculation.

4. **No control arm:** All 3 arms receive active intervention. There is no conventional-therapy-only control group, making it impossible to attribute changes to the interventions.

5. **Outcome measure confusion:** Uses SAQ-7 (Seattle Angina Questionnaire) to assess NYHA class — these are different instruments measuring different constructs. SAQ-7 measures angina-specific quality of life, not heart failure functional class.

6. **Status abandoned:** Trial appears abandoned — UNKNOWN status, no results posted, no publications, >3 years past primary completion.

7. **Undisclosed product identity:** The AFA supplement is almost certainly a Kalyagen/STEMREGEN commercial product based on the sister trial evidence, but this is not disclosed — raising transparency concerns.

8. **No published results:** Despite completion dates in 2022–2023, no results have been posted or published anywhere discoverable.

### Evidence Level

This trial, even if completed and published, would constitute **Level 4 evidence** (case series / pilot study) at best, given:
- No true control group
- Tiny sample size
- Open-label design
- Single center
- Undisclosed conflicts of interest
